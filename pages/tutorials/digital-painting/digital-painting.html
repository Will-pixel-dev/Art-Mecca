Digital Painting Fundamentals

Learn the essential techniques for creating stunning digital artwork from scratch.

45 min read
Sarah Johnson
March 15, 2023
Intermediate

Tools Required

  • Digital painting software (Photoshop, Procreate, Krita)
  • Graphics tablet or iPad
  • Basic brush set
Step-by-step digital painting process
0% complete~10 min remaining

1Introduction to Digital Painting

Digital painting combines traditional painting techniques with digital tools, offering artists unlimited possibilities for creativity. In this tutorial, we'll cover the fundamental techniques that every digital artist should master.

Pro Tip

Always work with a high-resolution canvas (at least 300 DPI) to ensure your artwork looks crisp when printed.

2Setting Up Your Workspace

An organized workspace is crucial for efficient digital painting. Customize your interface to have quick access to the tools you use most frequently.

Essential Panels

  • Layers Panel: Keep your artwork organized with separate layers
  • Brushes Panel: Quick access to your favorite brushes
  • Color Picker: Easily select and save custom colors
  • History: Track your progress and undo mistakes

3Brush Techniques and Settings

Mastering brushes is key to creating realistic digital paintings. Understand how different brush settings affect your strokes.

Opacity

Controls transparency of your strokes

Flow

Determines how quickly paint is applied

Hardness

Controls edge softness

Spacing

Affects stroke continuity

Pro Tip

Create custom brushes for specific textures like hair, foliage, or skin to speed up your workflow.

4Color Theory and Application

Understanding color relationships will dramatically improve your digital paintings. Learn how to create harmonious color schemes.

Pro Tip

Use overlay or soft light layers to add lighting effects without permanently altering your base painting.

5Lighting and Shadow

Proper lighting gives your paintings depth and dimension. Learn how to create realistic light sources and shadows.

Pro Tip

Use overlay or soft light layers to add lighting effects without permanently altering your base painting.
